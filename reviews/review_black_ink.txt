                                <h2 class="post-title">Which ink to choose for watercolor ?</h2>
                                <p class="post-meta">
                                Date : 05/01/2020 by <class="post-author">Alexandre Proux</a> under <a class="post-category post-category-design">REVIEW</a>
                                </p>
                            </header>
                            <div class="post-description">
								Black ink is widely used in watercolor to bring contrast to painting, as in the line and wash technique. To me, 3 main criteria must be considered 
to choose an ink : <u>water resistance</u>,
								<u>intensity of black</u> and <u>cloging</u> (can it be used with a fountain pen ?)
								Finding the perfect black ink for watercolor can be a real challenge. During my watercolor adventure, I accumulated many bottles of ink,
								in the rest of this article I will compare them and give you my opinions on each brand.
								
								<br>
								<br>
							    <div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">
                                <img width=100% height=100%
                                             src="../static/img/review_black_ink/review_black_ink_presentation.jpg"        
                                             alt="black_ink_presentation"></a>
                                </div>

								<br>
								<br>
								For this comparison, I have selected the following inks:
								<o1>
							    <li>Winsor and Newton black indian ink (951)</li>
								<li>Random ink bought in china</li>
                                <li>Hero 234</li>
								<li>Sennelier indian ink "a la pagode"</li>
								<li>Pelikan fount india</li>
								<li>Platinium carbon ink</li>
                                <li>Pigma Micro (0.8)</li>
								</o1>
								<br>
								
								My benchmark is composed of 3 test cases :
								<o1>
							    <li>A writing sample with 30 seconds of dry time</li>
								<li>A writing sample with 5 minutes of dry time</li>
								<li>A writing sample with 30 minutes of dry time</li>
								</o1>
							
								<br>
							
								<div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">
                                <img width=100% height=100%
                                             src="../static/img/review_black_ink/review_black_ink_test.jpg"        
                                             alt="black_ink_test"></a>
                                </div>
							
								<br>
								<br>
								Let's discuss about the results !
								<br>
								<br>
								<u>Winsor and Newton black indian ink (951)</u>
								<br>
								<o1>
							    <li>Water resistance : Very good </li>
								<li>Intensity of black : Very good </li>
								<li>Cloging : can't be used with a fountain pen</li>
								</o1>
								
								<br>
								<u>Random ink bought in china</u>
								<br>
								<o1>
							    <li>Water resistance : no resistance at all </li>
								<li>Intensity of black : greyish </li>
								<li>Cloging : can't be used with a fountain pen</li>
								</o1>
								
								<br>
								<u>Hero 234</u>
								<br>
								<o1>
							    <li>Water resistance : no resistance at all </li>
								<li>Intensity of black : good </li>
								<li>Cloging : some people on internet use this ink with fountain pen, i would not try it</li>
								</o1>
								
								<br>
								<u>Sennelier indian ink "a la pagode"</u>
								<br>
								<o1>
							    <li>Water resistance : Excellent when dry</li>
								<li>Intensity of black : best of the benchmark </li>
								<li>Cloging : really difficult to use, it clogs brushes and nib. This ink shall never be used with a fountain pen. I recommend diluting the ink with 
water to improve its fluidity. </li>
								</o1>

								<br>
								<u>Pelikan Fount india</u>
								<br>
								<o1>
							    <li>Water resistance : no resistance at all </li>
								<li>Intensity of black : good </li>
								<li>Cloging : compatible with fountain pen, but it should be cleaned regularly </li>
								</o1>								

								<br>
								<u>Platinium Carbon ink</u>
								<br>
								<o1>
							    <li>Water resistance : Excellent </li>
								<li>Intensity of black : Excellent </li>
								<li>Cloging : compatible with fountain pen, but it should be cleaned regularly </li>
								</o1>								
																
                                <br> 
								Conclusion ? There is a lot of black ink out there. Right now my favorite is the Platinium Carbon ink combine with my lamy 2000. 
								Note : Noodler sell a bullet-proof ink which has very good reviews, probably worth a try.
                                </p>
