                                <p class="post-meta">

                            <div class="post-description">

                                
                                <p>In November 2019, seduced by its design and its price, I bought the Hahnemühle watercolor book A5.
                                <br>Today, I've finished it, let's do a little review of this watercolor notebook ! </p>
                                
                                
                                <div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">
                                    <p><img src="../../static/img/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_5.gif" alt="review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_5" style="width:100%;height:100%;"></p>
                                </div>
                                <!-- end_header -->  
                                <p>
                                Choosing watercolor paper, is a difficult exercice, in particular when choosing a new watercolor book. Some criteria are to consider: 
                                <ul style="list-style-type: circle;">
                                <li>The price : watercolor paper is expensive, cheaper in notebook format</li>
                                <li>Paper weight : 200 g/m2 - 300 g/m2 - 400 g/m2 ? It depends on the amount of water used in your paints. </li>
                                <li>Durability : What is worst than notebook loosing sheets ? </li>
                                <li>Number of sheets : Usually 8 to 50 pages</li>
                                <li>Format : Portrait or landscape? A4, A5 or A6? </li>
                                <li>Material : Cellulose or cotton ? (Cotton is often preferred by watercolorists but is more expensive) </li>
                                </ul>
                                <p>
                                <div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">
                                    <p><img src="../../static/img/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_1.jpg" alt="review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_1" style="width:100%;height:100%;"></p>
                                </div>
                                <div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">
                                    <p><img src="../../static/img/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_2.jpg" alt="review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_2" style="width:100%;height:100%;"></p>
                                </div>
                                <p>&nbsp;</p>
                                <p>Well, the Hahnemühle is a serious candidate :</p>
                                <p>
                                <ul style="list-style-type: circle;">
                                <li>The price : good value for money at time of article </li>
                                <li>Paper weight : 200 g/m2 but supports surprisingly well water </li>
                                <li>Durability : Very solid, mine hasn't lost a page, the cover is still rigid </li>
                                <li>Number of sheets : 30 sheets and can be painted on both side</li>
                                <li>Format : Exists in portrait and landscape, A4/A5/A6? Comes with a bookmark and a rubber band to close it </li>
                                <li>Material : Cellulose, finish fine grain </li>
                                </ul>
                                </p>
                                <div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">
                                    <p><img src="../../static/img/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book/review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_3.jpg" alt="review_hahnemuhle_watercolor_book_3" style="width:100%;height:100%;"></p>
                                </div>
                                <p>&nbsp;</p>
                                <p> 
                                In use, it turns out to be a very good notebook. I find that it holds well wet techniques considering it has a weight of 200 g/m2. Little tips, use paper clip to reduce paper crinkling.  
                                </p>
