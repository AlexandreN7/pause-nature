                                <p>

                                Watercolorists are always struggling to find the perfect traveling palette. The tradeoff must be made according to :
                                <o1>
                                <li>Size (How much pans do you need ? Should it hold in your pocket ?)</li>
                                <li>Weight/material (Do you prefer metal or plastic?)</li>
                                <li>Mixing areas</li>
                                <li>Price (Brass palette can be very expensive)</li>
                                </o1>
                                <br>
                                Well the portable painter is a serious candidat.
								<!-- end_header -->
                                <br>
                                Main features :
                                <o1>
                                <li>Maker : Portable Painter (www.portablepainter.com)</li>
                                <li>Size : 14x7.5x2.5mm</li>
                                <li>Weight : 170g (without paint)</li>
                                <li>Material : plastic</li>
                                <li>Price : ~25-30â‚¬</li>
                                <li>Included : 12 empty half pans with sticky pad</li>
                                <li>Included : a synthetic double brush</li>
                                <li>Included : a silicon retaining band</li>
                                </o1>

                                <br>


                                <div class="photo-box pure-u-1 pure-u-md-1-2 pure-u-lg-1-3">

                                <br>
                                This palette principle is unique, it suppose to place on your knee/leg, and the two buckets filled with water. This allows you to have your both 
hands free for painting. Well... If you paint standing it will be less practical :).
                                <br>
                                The silicon band is suppose to hold things, such as : pencil, brush and the little metal piece that hold the palette together.

                                <br>
                                About the brush, the one included is a modest synthetic double brush. For the price of the palette, what could we expect ? Nevertheless, I had to 
make some changes to my portable painter palette in a way that my Escoda Reserva size 8 could fit in it.
                                Easy job, simply cut the plastic dividers !
