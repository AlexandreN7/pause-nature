Review : Hahnemühle watercolor book

Review : Hahnemühle watercolor book

Date : 17/01/2020 by Alexandre Proux under REVIEW

In November 2019, seduced by its design and its price, I bought the Hahnemühle watercolor book A5.
Today, I've finished it, let's do a little review of this watercolor notebook !

Choosing watercolor paper, is a difficult exercice, in particular when choosing a new watercolor book. Some criteria are to consider:

  • The price : watercolor paper is expensive, cheaper in notebook format
  • Paper weight : 200 g/m2 - 300 g/m2 - 400 g/m2 ? It depends on the amount of water used in your paints.
  • Durability : What is worst than notebook loosing sheets ?
  • Number of sheets : Usually 8 to 50 pages
  • Format : Portrait or landscape? A4, A5 or A6?
  • Material : Cellulose or cotton ? (Cotton is often preferred by watercolorists but is more expensive)

 

Well, the Hahnemühle is a serious candidate :

  • The price : good value for money at time of article
  • Paper weight : 200 g/m2 but supports surprisingly well water
  • Durability : Very solid, mine hasn't lost a page, the cover is still rigid
  • Number of sheets : 30 sheets and can be painted on both side
  • Format : Exists in portrait and landscape, A4/A5/A6? Comes with a bookmark and a rubber band to close it
  • Material : Cellulose, finish fine grain

 

In use, it turns out to be a very good notebook. I find that it holds well wet techniques considering it has a weight of 200 g/m2. Little tips, use paper clip to reduce paper crinkling.
