Which ink to choose for watercolor ?

Date : 05/01/2020 by Alexandre Proux under REVIEW

Black ink is widely used in watercolor to bring contrast to painting, as in the line and wash technique. To me, 3 main criteria must be considered to choose an ink : water resistance, intensity of black and cloging (can it used with a fountain pen ?) Finding the perfect black ink for watercolor can be a real challenge. During my watercolor adventure, I accumulated many bottles of ink, in the rest of this article I will compare them and give you my opinions on each brand.



For this comparison, I have selected the following inks:
  • Winsor and Newton black indian ink (951)
  • Random ink bought in china
  • Hero 234
  • Sennelier indian ink "a la pagode"
  • Pelikan fount india
  • Platinium carbon ink
  • Pigma Micro (0.8)
    •
    My benchmark is composed of 3 test cases :
  • A writing sample with 30 seconds of dry time
  • A writing sample with 5 minutes of dry time
  • A writing sample with 30 minutes of dry time
    •


    Let's discuss about the results !

    Winsor and Newton black indian ink (951)
  • Water resistance : Very good
  • Intensity of black : Very good
  • Cloging : can't be used with a fountain pen
    •
    Random ink bought in china
  • Water resistance : no resistance at all
  • Intensity of black : greyish
  • Cloging : can't be used with a fountain pen
    •
    Hero 234
  • Water resistance : no resistance at all
  • Intensity of black : good
  • Cloging : some people on internet use this ink with fountain pen, i would not try it
    •
    Sennelier indian ink "a la pagode"
  • Water resistance : Excellent when dry
  • Intensity of black : best of the benchmark
  • Cloging : really difficult to use, it clogs brushes and nib. This ink shall never be used with a fountain pen. I recommend diluting the ink with water to improve its fluidity.

    • Pelikan Fount india
  • Water resistance : no resistance at all
  • Intensity of black : good
  • Cloging : compatible with fountain pen, but it should be cleaned regularly

    • Platinium Carbon ink
  • Water resistance : Excellent
  • Intensity of black : Excellent
  • Cloging : compatible with fountain pen, but it should be cleaned regularly

    • Conclusion ? There is a lot of black ink out there. Right now my favorite is the Platinium Carbon ink combine with my lamy 2000. Note : Noodler sell a bullet-proof ink which has very good reviews, probably worth a try.
