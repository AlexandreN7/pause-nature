Review : Portable painter palette

Date : 01/01/2020 by Alexandre Proux under REVIEW

Watercolorists are always struggling to find the perfect traveling palette. The tradeoff must be made according to :

  • Size (How much pans do you need ? Should it hold in your pocket ?)
  • Weight/material (Do you prefer metal or plastic?)
  • Mixing areas
  • Price (Brass palette can be very expensive)

    • Well the portable painter is a serious candidat.
    Main features :
  • Maker : Portable Painter (www.portablepainter.com)
  • Size : 14x7.5x2.5mm
  • Weight : 170g (without paint)
  • Material : plastic
  • Price : ~25-30â‚¬
  • Included : 12 empty half pans with sticky pad
  • Included : a synthetic double brush
  • Included : a silicon retaining band
    •


    This palette principle is unique, it suppose to place on your knee/leg, and the two buckets filled with water. This allows you to have your both hands free for painting. Well... If you paint standing it will be less practical :).
    The silicon band is suppose to hold things, such as : pencil, brush and the little metal piece that hold the palette together.


    Comparison between palettes (24 pans and 48 pans).


    Pans are sold empty. Following pictures compare portable painter pan with Sennelier and Schmincke pan.


    About the brush, the one included is a modest synthetic double brush. For the price of the palette, what could we expect ? Nevertheless, I had to make some changes to my portable painter palette in a way that my Escoda Reserva size 8 could fit in it. Easy job, simply cut the plastic dividers !


    Conclusion ? It's my main travelling palette now :)
